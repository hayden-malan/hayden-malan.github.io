Some Thoughts After Watching Shereef's Fireside Chat:

The EDA Experience So Far:

My take on the EDA experience so far is highly positive. It seems almost too good to be true. What I was daydreaming about when stuck in exam rooms or on the construction site. It's being able to grow your own knowledge, work in a kind and helpful team of people who are just as dedicated as you are, and still be able to get a job after.

What I took away from Shereef's video:

What I took away from Shereef's video was the importance of the Kitchen Mindset, the feeling of being in charge of creating the work, and being able to actively participate throughthe ups and downs, the fun parts and the disasters.

How I see myself engaging in EDA culture:

I might be shy at first, but I think it is a supportive space for new learning and growth.

My expectations of EDA:

They have definitely changed. I thought it would be a lot more serious, and I was cynical about the teamwork side, but now that I've started I can see it really works.

How I feel about the learning environment:

I am excited, and nervous to learn in this environment, but it's all helping me to stay productive, so I don't worry about being nervous.
